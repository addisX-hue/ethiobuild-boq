BOQ Questions
A Bill of Quantities (BOQ) is a document that lists every material, work item, and quantity needed to construct a building, along with its unit rate and total cost. Contractors use it to price a project, and clients use it to understand exactly what they are paying for.
A cost estimate gives a single approximate total, often based on area or building type. A BOQ breaks that total into individual line items — excavation, concrete, rebar, blockwork, finishes — each with its own quantity and rate. A BOQ is more detailed and is usually required for tendering and contractor bidding.
At minimum: building use, built-up area, number of floors, foundation type, and finishing specifications (wall, roof, floor, paint). A detailed structural BOQ additionally needs approved drawings with column, beam, and slab dimensions.
A BOQ is used to request quotations from contractors, compare bids fairly on the same basis, track project costs during construction, and support loan or budget applications with a professional, itemized cost breakdown.
A Quick BOQ estimates cost from building type, area, and general specifications — useful for early budgeting in minutes. A Professional BOQ is calculated from actual approved structural drawings, giving precise quantities suitable for tendering and contractor agreements.
